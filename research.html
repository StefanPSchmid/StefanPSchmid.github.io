My research

In my research, I will work at the intersection of Chemistry and Artificial Intelligence. I will develop chemistry-informed algorithms that integrate domain-expert chemistry knowledge into AI algorithms. The goal of this work is to reduce the number of costly experiments that have to be carried out and facilitate the discovery of novel catalysts.
Prior to my doctoral research, I specialized on the spectroscopic study of catalytic systems. I utilized Electron Paramagnetic Resonance Techniques for the elucidation of the mechanism of catalyst deactivation through coke deposition in the zeolite-catalyzed methanol-to-olefin process (see this publication). Further, I contributed to the development of active site descriptors via Nuclear Magnetic Resonance for the Mo-catalyzed Olefin Metathesis (see this publication).
